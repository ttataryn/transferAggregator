Transfer Reliability Guide

TRANSFER RUMORS SIMPLIFIED

When it comes to transfers, Twitter is a widely popular resource, which continually churns out rumors.

The majority of these rumors have no basis, and may be there purely for clickbait and to attract more followers

So, after examining hundreds of transfers and their corresponding tweets the past couple of years, I have compiled the

Transfer Rumor Reliability Guide

Tier-Based System

  • Tier 0 - The official club Twitter or website.
  • Tier 1 - These are the highest-quality sources. May not be 100% accurate or even the first to call a transfer. However, if they are reporting on it, it is almost certainly true, or will go through in the near future.
  • Tier 2 - These are a step down from the Tier 1 and fall just short of being considered Tier 1 credible. However, they are known to have current and reliable contacts within their respective/corresponding clubs and their reports still may indicate a strong possibility of them occuring.
  • Tier 3 - Not very reliable, or average, especially compared to Tier 1 and 2. Some accounts in this category may fall into the click-bait tactics and are often fighting to be relevant among the sea of rumors and reporters.
  • Tier 4 -This tier includes the rest: the aggregators, indirect sources, and other secondhand accounts. These companies and accounts make a living out of ensuring that you read their tweets and they will make up nearly anything to get what they want.
